Until

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 14, 2026

Until is a macOS menubar app that shows your next Google Calendar event and helps you join meetings and take notes. It runs entirely on your Mac and talks directly to Google. There is no Until server, no account with us, and no data collection by us. This policy explains what Google user data the app accesses, how that data is used, with whom it is shared, how it is protected, and how it is retained and deleted.

What Google user data Until accesses

When you sign in with Google, Until requests the following OAuth scopes and accesses only the Google user data they cover:

  • Calendar list (calendar.calendarlist.readonly) — the names, colors, and IDs of the calendars in your account.
  • Calendar events (calendar.events) — event details such as title, time, attendees, location, conferencing links, and attachments.
  • Files the app itself creates in Google Drive (drive.file) — your meeting-notes documents and the folder that holds them. This scope only grants access to files and folders created by Until; the app cannot see, read, or list anything else in your Drive.
  • Your Google account email address (userinfo.email, openid) — the email address of the signed-in account.

Until does not access any other Google user data.

How Until uses Google user data

Until uses the data above only to provide the app’s user-facing features, at your direction:

  • Calendar list is used to let you choose which calendars to show or hide.
  • Calendar events are used to display your upcoming events in the menubar, open meeting links, and — only when you ask — to add a Google Meet link to an event or attach a meeting-notes document to it.
  • Drive files created by the app are used to create, name, and organize your meeting-notes documents, and — only when you ask — to share a notes document with the attendees of the meeting.
  • Your email address is used to show which account is connected and to identify you when sharing notes documents.

Until does not use Google user data for advertising, analytics, profiling, or any purpose other than the features described above.

Sharing, transfer, and disclosure of Google user data

Until does not share, transfer, or disclose Google user data to anyone. There is no Until server: the app makes direct API calls from your Mac to Google (Calendar, Drive, Docs, and sign-in), and nothing is routed through or stored on a server operated by us or any third party. Until does not sell, rent, or trade your data, and does not pass it to third-party services, ad networks, or data brokers.

The only time data moves anywhere is at your explicit direction within the app — for example, when you choose to share a meeting-notes document, Until asks Google Drive to grant access to the attendees you selected. That sharing happens inside your own Google account, under Google’s privacy policy.

How Until protects Google user data

  • Sensitive data is stored in the macOS Keychain. Your Google OAuth tokens — the most sensitive data the app holds — are stored in the macOS Keychain, encrypted and protected by the operating system like any other credential. They are never written to plain files or logs.
  • All network traffic is encrypted. Every request Until makes goes directly to Google’s APIs over HTTPS (TLS).
  • Data stays on your device. Calendar and event data is fetched on demand and kept in memory or in local app storage on your Mac, protected by your macOS user account. There is no server-side copy to breach.
  • Minimum scopes. Until requests only the scopes listed above, uses the read-only calendar-list scope, and uses the restricted-to-own-files drive.file scope instead of full Drive access.

Retention and deletion of Google user data

  • Calendar and event data is fetched on demand and held only in memory and a small local cache while you use the app. It is not accumulated or archived, and it is deleted when you remove the account or uninstall the app.
  • OAuth tokens are retained in the macOS Keychain only while your account is connected. Removing the account in Settings → Accounts deletes the tokens from your Keychain immediately.
  • Meeting-notes documents live in your own Google Drive and belong to you. Until keeps no copy; you can delete them in Drive at any time.
  • To delete all data the app holds: remove the account in Settings → Accounts (deletes tokens and cached data) and, if you wish, delete the app. Because Until has no server, there is no server-side data to request deletion of — removal on your Mac is complete deletion.

Google API Services User Data Policy and Limited Use

Until’s use of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. Data obtained from Google is used only to provide the user-facing features described above and is never transferred to others except at your direction to provide those features. Until does not use Google user data for advertising, does not allow humans to read it, and does not use Google user data — including Google Workspace data — to develop, improve, or train generalized or non-personalized AI and/or machine-learning models.

How to revoke access

You can disconnect Until at any time:

  • In the app, open Settings → Accounts and choose Remove to sign out and clear the stored tokens from your Keychain.
  • From your Google Account, visit Google Account permissions and remove Until’s access.

Changes to this policy

If the app’s data practices change, this page will be updated and the date at the top revised before the change takes effect.
